Why Scrim Instead of Ranked Solo Queue?

Solo queue MLBB ranked is the worst environment to actually improve in. You get throwers, AFKers, ML pickers ignoring counters, and supports who refuse to ward. Two hours of soloq teaches you almost nothing because the variables are completely random.

SGS scrims fix that. You play with the same five-stack repeatedly, against other Singaporean stacks at your tier. Communication is on Discord/Telegram voice. Drafts are real. Wins and losses come down to actual play, not your random duo's emotional state. This is how every Mythic Glory player in Singapore actually got there.

How SGS Scrims Work

  1. Join the Telegram. Click the Join Telegram button anywhere on this site. The channel is public; entry is free.
  2. Verify your account. Post your in-game ID, current rank and primary role. SGS verifies you're a real Singaporean player at the rank you claim — this is the smurf filter.
  3. Wait for a scrim window. Scrim openings are announced in the channel. Tag in if your rank fits. Most scrims fill in 5–10 minutes.
  4. Play, review, repeat. Each scrim is best-of-1 or best-of-3. After the game, players review the draft and key fights. This is the improvement loop solo queue never gives you.

Who Plays in SGS Scrims?

  • Mythic Glory — daily, evenings. Aspiring tournament players.
  • Mythic Honor — daily. The grind tier; most active.
  • Mythic — multiple times daily.
  • Legend / Epic — regular weekend slots. Good for players climbing for the first time.

Players come from across Singapore — students, working pros, ex-tournament players. Most stay in SGS for years because the alternative is ladder hell.
