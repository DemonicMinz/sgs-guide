Why Teaming Up in Singapore Is Hard (And Why SGS Fixes It)

Random matchmaking in MLBB is a coin flip. Half your games are decided in pick/ban. The other half are decided by whether your gold-lane marksman is tilted from his last loss. If you want to actually climb, you need teammates who:

  • Speak your language and are awake at your hours
  • Play their assigned role instead of insta-locking jungle
  • Don't AFK at -3 KDA
  • Will review losses with you instead of blaming the matchmaker

SGS exists because random matchmaking can't give you any of that. The Telegram channel is filtered for verified Singaporean players, sorted by rank, with role-fill posts going up daily.

Common Roles in Demand

These are the roles SGS sees the most "team looking for X" posts for:

  • Roam / Tank — perpetual shortage. Tigreal/Mathilda/Edith mains get tryouts within hours.
  • Support — high demand, especially for utility supports (Floryn, Estes, Mathilda).
  • EXP Lane — moderate demand. Versatile pools (tank+fighter dual-role) are most wanted.
  • Mid Lane — competitive but doable; mages with deep pool (3+ proven heroes) stand out.
  • Jungle — most contested role. Tournament-level junglers sometimes wait weeks for the right team fit.
  • Gold Lane / Marksman — high supply. Stand out by mastering 4+ marksmen across the meta.

How to Get Picked Up Faster

  1. Be honest about your rank. Smurfs get filtered out fast. Post your real current rank and best peak.
  2. Show your hero pool. Three-deep on a single role beats eleven heroes spread across all five.
  3. State your hours. "Mythic, support, on after 9 PM SGT" gets replies. "Looking for team" doesn't.
  4. Scrim before joining a team. Use SGS scrim windows to test chemistry before committing — both you and the team are evaluating fit.
